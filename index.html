What is the difference between MP3 and WAV?

MP3 is a lossy compressed format that permanently discards audio data to reduce file size. WAV is uncompressed and preserves the complete audio signal. WAV files are larger but maintain full fidelity, making them the standard for professional audio production. Read our full comparison of WAV vs MP3 for music production.
